Timetable · April–May tasksHover to open

April — Autumn tasks

✓
Now · Citrus
Plant companion crops around citrus
Nasturtiums, comfrey and borage near citrus improve soil fertility and attract beneficial insects.
✓
Now · Fertilising
Apply citrus fertiliser — second autumn window
Autumn is the second fertiliser window. Slow-release balanced fertiliser before winter slowdown.
›
After leaf drop · Figs
Prune fig trees
Remove crossed branches and open the centre for airflow. Keep dormant pruning minimal to avoid sap loss.
›
Autumn · Perennials
Divide and replant tractor seat plants (Ligularia)
Autumn is ideal for dividing Ligularia. Replant in damp shaded spots with compost amendment.
›
Now · Veg bed
Start winter vegetable beds
Sow broad beans, peas, kale, silverbeet and spinach. Prepare beds with compost before soil cools.
›
General · Mulching
Apply thick mulch before winter
Straw or wood chip mulch retains moisture and insulates roots through Adelaide's winter.

May — Coming up

›
May · Planting
Garlic planting
May is the prime garlic planting window in Adelaide. Plant cloves 10cm deep, 15cm apart in well-drained soil.
›
May–Jun · Citrus
Protect cold-sensitive citrus
Limes and lemons may need frost cloth on cold nights. Move potted citrus to sheltered positions.
›
Compost
Turn compost piles
Turn piles to accelerate breakdown before adding winter organic matter. Good time to add autumn leaves.
